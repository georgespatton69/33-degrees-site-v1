What Are Peptides?

A peptide is a short chain of amino acids — the same building blocks that make up proteins. Peptides act like biological signals or messengers that tell the body to do very specific things at the cellular level.

Think of them like a text message between cells telling your body what to do.

Depending on the peptide, they can:

Help with healing and tissue repair
Support muscle growth
Improve skin, collagen, and anti-aging
Regulate hormones
Assist with fat loss and metabolism
Influence immune function

What makes peptides powerful:

Targeted They do one specific job
Efficient Small and easily used by the body
Natural Mimic what your body already produces

Regulatory Shifts

FDA & Research Framework

The regulatory environment continues to evolve as research peptides gain mainstream attention. Compounding pharmacies and research suppliers are adapting to new compliance frameworks while maintaining accessibility for qualified research.

Clinical Progress

From Lab to Application

Retatrutide completed Phase 3 trials showing 23.7% body weight reduction — the most effective GLP-1 receptor agonist to date. BPC-157 and TB-500 continue to accumulate evidence for tissue repair applications.

Longevity Science

The Anti-Aging Frontier

NAD+ precursors and mitochondrial peptides like SS-31 and MOTS-c are at the forefront of aging research. Epithalon's telomere-supporting properties continue to attract serious scientific interest.

What's Next

The Road Ahead
